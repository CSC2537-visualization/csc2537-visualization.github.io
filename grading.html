RESEARCH PROJECT (50 pts)

Refer to the Project page for more details this assignment.

Students will complete a mini-research project in groups of two. We encourge you to choose projects that are related to your own research or another research project on campus. Leveraging ongoing research increases your speed and ability to find define a good project, and enables you to leverage existing software and hardware infrastructure. The grade for this portion will be broken up as follows:

Project Abstract Draft (including Research Question) - 5 pts
The strength of the research problem (i.e., do pick something that's research, not just development) and appropriateness of the scope (i.e., don't pick something too large â€” it's only a 12-week term.) Is it executed in ways necessary to understand your research question? (It doesn't need to be functional in ways orthogonal to your research question.)
Mid-term presentation - 10 pts
A short presentation to the class explaining your problem motivation, research question, research methodology and progress to date
Final Report, including execution - 25 pts
Explanation of your ideas, motivation, and related work. How well did you figure out what you set out to learn?
Project Presentation - 10 pts
A presentation of your research results to the class (and possibly outside guests).

In general, both members of the team will receive the same grade. However, if there is a significant contribution disparity, that will be reflected in the grade.

PAPER COMMENTARIES (20 pts)

Refer to the Commentaries page for more details about this assignment.

Students are required to read all the papers listed in the syllabus. Commentary submissions are only required for those marked on the syllabus.

We will drop your lowest-scoring commentary. This implies each student may opt to pass on one commentary for any reason: personal or family matters, conflicting deadlines, etc.. There are no exemptions beyond this. When evaluating commentaries, we will look at organization, conciseness and relevance of the discussion, as well as whether the discussion includes critical thoughts and personal reflection. Commentaries are graded on a check-plus, check, check-minus scale:

Excellent (5 pts) Organized, concise and relevant discussion that develops critical thoughts and personal reflection.
Satisfactory (3 pts) Discussion is relevant, but lacks depth and/or is not quite well organized / lacks conciseness and clarity.
Unsatisfactory (1 pt) Discussion is too short, too basic, off-topic and/or difficult to understand.

LEADING ONE CLASS DISCUSSION (30 pts)

Refer to the Discussion page for instructions on how to prepare for this assignment.

Students are required to read a research paper in depth, and lead a class discussion on the research question, contributions, limitations and conclusions of the paper (and beyond). In evaluating your discussion, the course instructor will ask the following questions:

▪ Did the discussion moderator effectively extract the high-level points from readings and present them?
▪ Did the discussion moderator raise interesting and difficult questions about the readings?
▪ Did the discussion moderator clearly review, describe, and summarize the paper's main technical contributions? If there is an important methodology or algorithm, you should explain how it works. If there are statistics, you should explain how and why they were produced.
▪ Did the discussion moderator demonstrate critical analysis of the paper's content?
▪ Did the discussion moderator discussion accomplish valuable learning goals for the audience? (i.e., did students understand something about the readings at the end of class that may not have at the beginning.)
▪ Did the discussion moderator engage the students in discussion?

This is how your grade out of 30 is determined:

Weak (0-16 pts) Yes to few of the questions above
Proficient (17-25 pts) Yes to most but not all of the questions above
Mastery (26-30 pts) Yes to all of the questions above
